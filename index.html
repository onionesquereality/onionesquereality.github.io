                I am a machine learning researcher with broad interests in the theory and practice of machine learning. For the past few years my research has focused on developing rigorous theoretical and engineering tools for data-efficient machine learning and enabling their safe and reliable deployment in real-world applications and decision-making pipelines. I often get inspiration from and work on applications in the physical sciences, science automation, and healthcare.
I have extensive experience in both academic and industrial research, including in the deployment of large-scale machine learning systems. I have built collaborations with and enjoy working with physicists, chemists, biologists, and industry healthcare & pharmaceuticals professionals. I have also been involved with multiple startups. For the moment, my focus has been on the development of geometric deep learning methods, conformal prediction procedures for uncertainty quantification, and neural network analysis using tools from statistical physics. My industrial work has been in the core healthcare and pharmaceutical space and has involved deploying large-scale models and working with tera-byte-level data. Much of my recent work on reliable machine learning stems from my industrial work. I have also been involved in a large, multiple institute collaboration on a robot scientist, which has further served to ground my core machine learning interests. My PhD research on equivariant neural networks is now foundational to the rapidly growing area of geometric deep learning. My work is at the crossroads of group theory, representation theory, harmonic analysis, probability theory, scientific computing, healthcare systems, and the physical sciences.
